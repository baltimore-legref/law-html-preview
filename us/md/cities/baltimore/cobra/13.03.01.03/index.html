.03 Prohibited Wild, Exotic, Hybrid, and Livestock Animals.

A. A person may not keep, or allow to be kept on that person's premises, a wild, exotic, hybrid, or livestock animal as specified in §B(1)-(27) of this regulation.

B. The following wild, exotic, hybrid, and livestock animals are prohibited:

(1) A wild, exotic, hybrid, or livestock animal whose possession or sale is prohibited by a federal, State, or City law, rule, or regulation;

(2) Dogs other than a domesticated dog (Canis familaris);

(3) Cats other than a domesticated cat (Felis catus);

(4) Bears;

(5) Bats;

(6) Squirrels;

(7) Fur-bearing mammals of the family Mustelidae other than a domesticated ferret (Mustela fero);

(8) Venomous insects other than honey bees;

(9) Spiders;

(10) Scorpions;

(11) Centipedes;

(12) Large rodents (Rodentia);

(13) Even-toed ungulates (Artiodactyla) other than a domesticated goat of the Miniature, Dwarf, or Pygmy breed;

(14) Odd-toed ungulates (Perissodactyla) other than a domesticated horse (Equus caballus) used in the Arabber or carriage trades or used by federal, State, or local law enforcement units;

(15) Marsupialia;

(16) Procyonidae;

(17) Carnivorous mammals of the family Viverridae;

(18) Non-human primates;

(19) Bovinae (cattle);

(20) Pigs, boars, and swine other than a domesticated Vietnamese Pot-bellied pig;

(21) Rabbits of the family Leporidae other than a domesticated rabbit that has been bred and supervised in a properly maintained rabbitry;

(22) Crocodylia;

(23) Venomous reptiles that are harmful to people, even if the reptile has had venom producing glands or structures removed;

(24) Snakes capable of growing to more than 5 feet when fully grown;

(25) Lizards capable of growing to more than 4 feet when fully grown;

(26) Green iguanas (Iguana iguana) less than 30 inches long or more than 6 feet long when fully grown; and

(27) Fowl other than domesticated hens and pigeons.

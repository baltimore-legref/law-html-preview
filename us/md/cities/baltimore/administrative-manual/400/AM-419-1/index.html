AM-419-1. Budget Account Number

SCOPE

The budget account number is a 26-digit control number divided into sets of digits and assigned to all City budgeted transactions. It is used to clarify and charge an agency's expenditures and appropriations. A budget account number is divided into five (5) sections, each of which can be specifically identified. The budget account number used to be a 15-digit control number. A description of both the old and the new types of account numbers appears below.

FUND TYPE

The first 4 digits of the account number identify the type of Fund from which resources will be made available for the operation of the program. Funds are categorized according to the following designations:

1001 – General Fund

2000 – Internal Service

2022 – Convention Center Bond Fund

2024 – Conduit Enterprise Fund

2070 – Waste Water Utility

2071 – Water Utility

2072 – Stormwater Utility

2073 – Loan & Guarantee Enterprise

2075 – Parking Enterprise

2076 – Parking Management

3001 – Motor Vehicle

4000 – Federal

5000 – State

6000 - Special

Since Fiscal 2013, Motor Vehicle funds have been combined with the General Fund for purposes of City appropriation; the City continues to report on all services and activities supported through State highway users revenues. For a description of each fund type, please refer to AM-420-4.

XXXX-xxxxxx-xxxx-xxxxxx-xxxxxx

DETAILED FUND and GRANT YEAR

The fifth through eighth digits identify the detailed fund. For all General, Parking, and Utility funds, this segment is identified with a generic code of "0000". For federal, State, and special grant funds, this four digit segment is a unique identifier for an individual grant. The first digit of the detailed fund will match the first digit of the fund number (ex: 4000-4123xx or 5000-5678xx)

xxxx-XXXXxx-xxxx-xxxxxx-xxxxxx

The next two digits identify the grant year. For each detailed grant, this segment allows for identification of the grant year when revenues are received or expenditures are charged (ex. – "4000-412315" would indicate a grant year of 2015. General, Parking, and Utility Funds again are identified through a generic code of "00" since accounts are appropriated on an annual basis.

xxxx-xxxxXX-xxxx-xxxxxx-xxxxxx

SERVICE

The eleventh through fourteenth digits designate the service. Each service number should have a unique identifier and correspond with a single agency.

xxxx-xxxxxx-XXXX-xxxxxx-xxxxxx

ACTIVITY AND SUB-ACTIVITY

The fifteenth through eighteenth digits will identify an activity. Each activity should have a unique four-digit designation and correspond with a single service.

xxxx-xxxxxx-xxxx-XXXXxx-xxxxxx

The following two digits (nineteenth and twentieth) will identify a sub activity. Few budgets are appropriated at a sub-activity level, but this designation may assist in identifying additional cost centers within an activity for accounting purposes. If a sub-activity is not budgeted, it is identified with a generic "00" code.

xxxx-xxxxxx-xxxx-xxxxXX-xxxxxx

NATURAL (OBJECT AND SUB-OBJECT)

The twenty-first through the twenty-sixth digits identify the natural account. This segment provides information on the line item identification. Expenditure accounts will begin with a "6", while revenue accounts will begin with a "4". The object of expenditure will be the 3rd digit in this sequence, and the sub-object of expenditure will be the last two digits in this code.

A list of the objects of expenditure is available in AM-420-4. A detailed list of the current natural expenditure account designations is available from BBMR upon request.

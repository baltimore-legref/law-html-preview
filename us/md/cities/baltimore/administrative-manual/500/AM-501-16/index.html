AM-501-16. Motor Vehicle - Acquisition and Disposal

Scope

The Department of General Services' Fleet Management Division (FMD) is responsible for the acquisition and disposal of motorized vehicles and related equipment that are owned by the Mayor and City Council (i.e. City-owned). The following procedures are to be used by all agencies involved in the requesting, operating, purchasing and disposal of the City's vehicular assets.

I.

Introduction

Vehicles are introduced into the fleet of City-owned vehicles either as replacements for assets being disposed of, or as additions to the existing fleet. The process for each of these routes of introducing vehicles to the fleet is different, and is outlined in this Policy.

A.

Replacement

FMD maintains a Fleet Replacement Plan, which is a 20-year projection of which vehicles are recommended to be replaced in each fiscal year. The plan is based primarily upon age, maintenance and repair cost history, mileage and utilization.

Based on this plan, and in conjunction with Agencies and their needs, FMD identifies specific vehicles to be disposed of. These "turn-ins" are removed from service once their replacement vehicles have been purchased, received and are ready for use.

B.

Addition to the Fleet

Under certain circumstances, agencies have a need to purchase vehicles to add to their existing fleet. Such circumstances could include: creation of a new unit, addition of a new service delivery program, etc. In these situations, the vehicle need was not anticipated by the City and therefore not in the FMD 20-year Replacement Plan.

The process for adding vehicles to the City's fleet is as follows:

1.

Agency completes a "Request for Addition to City Fleet of Mobile Equipment" Form (see attachment #Xxxx)

2.

Agency submits form to The Department of Finance's Bureau of Budget Management and Research (BBMR) for determination of affordability

3.

If supported by BBMR Analyst, permission is sought from Finance Director for the Fleet Addition

4.

Agency provides approval letter to DGS BBMR Analyst who will forward to FMD to initiate procurement

II.

Permanent Acquisition

A.

Financed Purchase, aka "Master Lease Purchase"

The process for purchasing vehicles using the capital-financing securing by the Department of Finance's Treasury Management Bureau is as follows:

1.

Annually, FMD identifies vehicles to be replaced, per the Fleet Replacement Plan

2.

Annually, FMD meets with agencies to review list and discuss program changes that may affect vehicle/equipment needs

3.

Agency prioritizes vehicles on list of potential replacements

4.

Department of Finance advises FMD of overall purchase spending limits, and for each agency

5.

FMD provides final list with estimated cost to BBMR for CLS budgeting

6.

BBMR builds costs into Agency budgets

7.

Upon approval by the Board of Estimates, Treasury Management Bureau secures financing

8.

FMD initiates procurement process

9.

Department of Finance's Bureau of Purchases procures vehicles

10.

FMD receives new vehicles and performs upfitting, tag & title, etc

11.

Agencies turn in vehicle to be replaced when picking up new vehicle. (If not turning in an asset, see Section I.B: Additions to the Fleet)

B.

Non-Financed Purchase, aka "Non-Master Lease Purchase"

Agencies may also purchase vehicles using funds other than the capital-financing securing by the Department of Finance's Treasury Management Bureau. Such funds could include agency allocations, enterprise funds, grant funds, etc. The process for the purchase of vehicles and equipment using these types of funds is as follows:

Agency Funds

1.

Annually, FMD identifies vehicles to be replaced, per the Fleet Replacement Plan

2.

Annually, FMD meets with agencies to review list and discuss program changes that may affect vehicle/equipment needs

3.

Agency prioritizes vehicles on list of potential replacements

4.

Department of Finance advises FMD of purchase spending limits for the specific agency

OR

1.

Agency identifies need for a vehicle/equipment

2.

Agency identifies vehicle/equipment to be turned in when new asset is received. (If not turning in an asset, see Section I.B: Additions to the Fleet)

3.

Agency consults FMD to determine costs of purchasing, operating and maintaining vehicle/equipment

4.

Agency provides cost information to BBMR for determination of affordability and to certify availability of funds

THEN,

5.

BBMR identifies Budget Account Number

6.

Agency acknowledges that if it does not have a reoccurring source of funds, it may not be able to replace the vehicle in the future using General Funds

7.

Agency requests permission from Finance Director for Non-Master Lease (NML) Purchase

8.

Agency provides approval letter to DGS BBMR Analyst who will forward to FMD to initiate procurement

9.

FMD finalizes vehicle specifications and begins procurement process

10.

Bureau of Purchases procures vehicles/equipment

11.

FMD receives new vehicles and performs upfitting, tag & title, etc

12.

Agency turns in vehicle to be replaced when picking up new vehicle. (If not turning in an asset, see Section I.B: Additions to the Fleet)

Grant Funds

1.

Agency identifies need for a vehicle/equipment; notification shall be provided to FMD no later than six (6) months prior to the expiration of the grant funding.

2.

Agency identifies vehicle/equipment to be turned in when new asset is received. (If not turning in an asset, see Section I.B: Additions to the Fleet)

3.

Agency consults FMD to determine costs of operating and maintaining vehicle/equipment

4.

Agency provides cost information to BBMR for determination of affordability and certify availability of funds

5.

BBMR identifies Budget Account Number

6.

Agency acknowledges that if it does not have a reoccurring source of funds, it may not be able to replace the vehicle in the future using General Funds

7.

Agency requests permission from Finance Director for Non-Master Lease (NML) Purchase

8.

Agency provides approval letter to DGS BBMR Analyst who will forward to FMD to initiate procurement

9.

FMD finalizes vehicle specifications and begins procurement process

10.

Bureau of Purchases procures vehicles

11.

FMD receives new vehicles and performs upfitting, tag & title, etc

12.

Agency turns in vehicle to be replaced when picking up new vehicle. (If not turning in an asset, see Section I.B: Additions to the Fleet)

C.

Vehicle/Equipment Donation

City Agencies may accept donation of vehicles and equipment from non-City entities, with prior approval from the Department of Finance. The process for introducing these donated assets into the City's fleet is as follows:

1.

Agency identifies or is offered a vehicle/equipment to be donated to the City

2.

Agency identifies vehicle/equipment to be turned in when new asset is received. (If not turning in an asset, see Section I.B: Additions to the Fleet)

3.

Agency consults FMD to determine costs of placing into service, operating and maintaining the vehicle/equipment

5.

Agency provides FMD's cost information to BBMR for determination of affordability

6.

If supported by BBMR Analyst, authorization to proceed is given to agency and FMD

4.

Agency receives vehicle/equipment and transports it immediately to FMD (or vehicle delivered to FMD)

5.

FMD receives new vehicles and performs upfitting, tag & title, etc

6.

Agency turns in vehicle to be replaced when picking up new vehicle. (If not turning in an asset, see Section I.B: Additions to the Fleet)

D.

Vehicle/Equipment Confiscation or Seizure

Certain City Agencies have the legal authority to confiscate or seize vehicles/equipment from private citizens or entities via civil forfeiture. The process for introducing these assets into the City's fleet is as follows:

1.

Agency identifies a vehicle/equipment to be seized/confiscated

2.

Agency identifies vehicle/equipment to be turned in when asset is identified. (If not turning in an asset, see Section I.B: Additions to the Fleet)

3.

Agency consults FMD to determine costs of placing into service, operating and maintaining the vehicle/equipment

4.

Agency provides FMD's cost information to BBMR for determination of affordability

5.

If supported by BBMR Analyst, authorization to proceed is given to agency and FMD

6.

Agency receives vehicle/equipment and transports it immediately to FMD (or vehicle delivered to FMD)

7.

FMD receives new vehicles and performs upfitting, tag & title, etc

8.

Agency turns in vehicle to be replaced when picking up new vehicle. (If not turning in an asset, see Section I.B: Additions to the Fleet)

III.

Temporary Acquisition

A.

Rentals/Leases

(Refer to AM-501-9 for additional guidance)

1.

Agency identifies vehicle/equipment need.

2.

The following procedures are to be used by each City Agency in requesting the purchase or rental/lease of vehicles.

3.

Agency head initiates completion and submission of a Requisition, utilizing the CitiBuy system with justification for the purchase of a new vehicle indicated on the "Notes" section of the CitiBuy requisition.

4.

BBMR Agency Budget Analyst reviews Requisition(s) in CitiBuy, certifies funds are available, and submits a hard copy to the Director of Finance for approval.

5.

If approved and signed by the Director of Finance, BBMR stamps a copy of the requisition and BBMR forwards it to the Bureau of Purchases.

6.

If request is disapproved, all documents submitted should be returned to the agency/bureau head with reason for denial.

7. APPEAL PROCESS:

To appeal, the agency/bureau head should send a memo to the Director of Finance, 469 City Hall, outlining the reasons for appeal. The Finance Director will notify agency/bureau head of the decision. Original documents must accompany the appeal. If approved, the documents will be considered as outlined in this policy or returned with reasons for denial.

8.

Agency receives vehicle

9.

Agency provides necessary information to FMD on a Motorized Equipment Status Report (MESR), for record creation if vehicle will be fueled at City facilities.

  • Upon turn in of rented/leased unit, Agency advises FMD to remove from records. (If not advised, FMD will not be responsible for charges against the wrong budget account number)

IV.

Inter-Agency Transfers

A.

Fleet Condition Trade

1.

FMD identifies vehicle/equipment belonging to "Agency A" that is being taken out of service for replacement, whose condition is better than a similar asset in fleet of "Agency B"

2.

FMD advises Agency B of the availability of an asset in better condition.

3.

The asset is transferred to Agency B, whose existing asset is taken out of service.

B.

Agency Identified Trade

1.

Agency identifies vehicle/equipment to be transferred to another agency

2.

Agency notifies FMD to change budget account numbers and contact information. (If not advised, FMD will not be responsible for charges against the wrong budget account number)

V.

Vehicle Disposal

Upon turn-in, FMD prepares vehicles/equipment for disposal following the Department of Finance's surplus property processes.

A.

Totaled Vehicles

Master Lease Purchased:

1.

Vehicle is taken out of service and disposed of

2.

If not fully paid off, agency continues to pay debt service on vehicles for the life of the loan.

3.

If vehicle must be replaced and funds are available in agency, new vehicle can be replaced as a Non-Master Lease purchase

4.

If vehicle must be replaced and funds are not available in agency, vehicle will be placed on the agency's Master Lease Replacement Plan for the following year.

Non-Master Lease Purchased:

1.

Vehicle is taken out of service and disposed of

2.

If vehicle must be replaced and funds are available in agency, new vehicle can be replaced as NML (See Section II.B: Non-Financed Purchase, aka "Non-Master Lease Purchase")

VI.

Terminology

Addition: Acquisition of an asset for which another will not be turned in for disposal and removal from the City's fleet.

Asset: As it pertains to vehicles and motorized equipment, one that is owned and operated by the City of Baltimore, for which a depreciation expense is assessed.

FMD: Fleet Management Division

Lease: Procurement of a vehicle or piece of motorized equipment for a temporary period, from an approved vendor with an existing Citibuy contract. Generally measured in months and distinguished from a Rental by the contract terms.

Maintenance Cost: Amount paid by agencies for the maintenance and repair of the asset. For example, services performed by Fleet Management Division, services performed by outside repair vendors, Parts issued for repair, etc.

Master Lease: Purchase of a vehicle/piece of motorized equipment using the City's debt-financed asset purchase program.

Non Master Lease: Purchase of a vehicle/piece of motorized equipment not using the Master Lease, for example, purchases funded by an agency, an Enterprise Fund, a grant, the Mobile Equipment Fund, etc.

Operating Cost: Amount paid by agencies for the use of the asset. For example, fuel consumed, Emissions testing, administrative charges from the Fleet Management Division, etc.

Purchase Cost: Amount paid to a third party for the acquisition of an asset.

Rental: Procurement of a vehicle or piece of motorized equipment for a temporary period, from an approved vendor with an existing CitiBuy contract. Generally measured in months and distinguished from a Lease by the contract terms.

Replacement: Acquisition of an asset for which another will be turned in for removal from the fleet.
